About the game

The Ghost of Redstone Manor is a first person murder mystery game where the player assumes the ghost form of the murdered writer Patricia Rotstein. The player makes use of multiple abilities to navigate the scene of the crime and attempt to solve the mystery. This project was conpleted over the course of 3 months as the final project of my Interactive Digital Media Masters in Trinity College Dublin. The game was built with a team of 4 using the Unreal Engine. I was the main programmer in the team.

My Work

I implemented and programmed all gameplay elements in the Ghost of Redstone Manor. I also participated heavily in the designing of the various gameplay elements.

Player Mechanics

Player movement and first person camera controls were provided by the Unreal Engine "First Person Starter" package. I built upon these core player controls. The player begins the game as ghost and can use various powers while in the forms. Players can then possess NPCs to take a physical form in the game. The player mechanics that I developed include:

  • Ghost form:
    • Walking through walls
    • Teleporting between house floors
    • Picking up small objects and throwing them
    • Possessing and controlling human NPCs
  • Human form:
    • Talking to other NPCs
    • Collecting objects & clues
    • Interacting with puzzles

Dialogue

A major feature in the Ghost of Redstone Manor is the ability to converse with NPCs while in human form. This enables players to gather clues, learn about the circumstances surrounding their character's life, and progress the storyline.

The dialogue system was the most intricate and involved aspect of my work load for the game. The key features and challenges for this system were:

  • Creating a scalable dialogue system that is fast and easy to use for creating new dialogue scenarios.
  • Progressing conversations based on the player's dialogue choices.
  • Dynamically changing the conversation with an NPC depending on the character that the player is currently possessing.
  • Dynamically updating conversations depending on player progress through the game
  • Triggering events and reactions based on player actions in dialogue such as giving important quest items to specific characters.

After testing different methods for this system, I settled on utilising Unreal's "Behaviour Tree" systems to build the dialogue. I created multiple customised Behaviour Tree function nodes to be used to create conversations. This is implemented together with a multitude of custom "decorator" nodes to check and alter the progress of the given dialogue tree based on factors such as who the player is possessing, what point of the game's progress the player is, and checking for scenario triggers.

Inventory

The player can find and collect quest objects that provide clues or enable the progress of the game. This is tracked using text references to track and assign key items. Items that are collected by the player can be viewed in an inventory menu that I developed and designed. Selecting an object in the menu will display the correscponding model and update the information container.

Animations

I implemented all the animations in the game. These include walking, talking, falling, and idle animations for NPCs. Furthermore, I implemented the first person perspective animations when possessing an NPC. This was accomplished with the animation blueprint system in Unreal Engine.

Environments & Effects

I conceptualised and programmed the environmental effects which occur when the player is in ghost form, as well as the dynamic switching between the visuals.

The house that the game takes place in is built twice. Once with the normal effects, and another with the ghost visuals. These seperate instances are stored in "Layers" that are toggled on and off depending on the player's current form. NPCs and quest objects are treated seperately within their own blueprints to shuffle between their different visuals.

AI Behaviour

I programmed the behaviours of the various NPCs. I used a behaviour tree system to determine their state and actions then update them. These actions include walking back to their original position after being possessed, and playing character specific idle animations such as sitting down, drinking from a glass, or smoking.
